    Company overview

    ANZ Group Holdings Limited is one of Australia‚Äôs major banking institutions and a leading financial services provider in the Asia Pacific region. It offers retail, commercial, agricultural, institutional and wealth management products to customers in Australia, New Zealand and internationally.

    The group structure separates ANZ Group Holdings as the listed parent from underlying regulated banking entities. ANZ describes itself as a bank that enables trade and capital flows across the region, with a particular focus on Australia, New Zealand and selected Asian markets. Operations span everyday retail banking, small business and agribusiness lending, through to complex institutional financial solutions.

    Business segments

    Personal banking

    • Offers transaction and savings accounts, debit and credit cards, and personal loans.
    • Provides home loans and mortgage products through branches, brokers and digital channels.
    • Delivers basic insurance and wealth offerings for retail and private banking customers.
    • Services accessed primarily via the ANZ mobile app, internet banking and national branch and ATM network.

    Business and agri

    • Provides lending, overdrafts and asset finance for small and medium enterprises.
    • Offers specialist finance for agricultural customers including seasonal working capital and equipment funding.
    • Includes merchant services, payment terminals and business transaction accounts.
    • Focuses on relationship banking through dedicated managers and regional teams.

    Institutional banking

    • Delivers loan products including syndicated loans, project and export finance, and acquisition financing.
    • Provides debt structuring, securitisation, capital markets and balance sheet solutions.
    • Offers working capital and liquidity products, cash management, deposits and clearing services.
    • Includes risk management services in foreign exchange, interest rates, commodities and credit.
    • Supports multinational corporates, financial institutions, government entities and large local corporates.

    Products and services

    ANZ provides its services through a mix of digital platforms and physical channels:

    • Internet banking and smartphone applications for everyday retail and business transactions.
    • A network of branches and ATMs in Australia and New Zealand.
    • Mortgage specialists, business bankers and private banking teams for more complex needs.
    • Contact centres for customer support across products and markets.
    • Global institutional banking teams covering trade, markets, cash management and corporate finance.

    Global footprint

    ANZ operates across multiple regions and positions itself as a bank connecting customers to global trade and capital flows.

    • Core markets: Australia and New Zealand.
    • Asia and Pacific: presence in markets such as Singapore, Hong Kong, China, Vietnam, India and Pacific Island nations.
    • Europe and United Kingdom: operations supporting institutional and wholesale clients.
    • North America: representative and wholesale banking activities.

    This international exposure differentiates ANZ from some domestic peers and creates both opportunities and additional regulatory and risk management obligations.

    Corporate governance

    • ANZ Group Holdings Limited is listed on the Australian Securities Exchange and subject to Australian prudential regulation through APRA and ASIC.
    • ISS governance quality score as at 1 November 2025 is 6, with:
      • Audit: 10
      • Board: 1
      • Shareholder rights: 1
      • Compensation: 7
    • The scores suggest relatively strong board structure and shareholder rights, with weaker outcomes in audit and remuneration dimensions.
    • Board oversight covers risk management, regulatory compliance, conduct, and climate and sustainability issues.
    • As a large bank, ANZ has been subject to heightened scrutiny following the Australian Royal Commission into misconduct in the financial sector and continues to implement remediation and conduct improvements.

    Risk and compliance considerations

    • Regulatory risk: multiple regulators across Australia, New Zealand, Asia, Europe and the United States oversee capital, liquidity, conduct, AML and prudential standards.
    • Credit and market risk: exposure to housing markets, business lending, institutional portfolios and traded markets activities.
    • Conduct and remediation: legacy issues in fees for no service, advice and product design, with associated customer remediation programmes.
    • Operational and cyber risk: reliance on digital channels and data systems creates exposure to outages, cyber attacks and data breaches.
    • Climate and transition risk: financed emissions in carbon intensive sectors create strategic, regulatory and reputational risk.

    Sustainability and ESG themes

    Climate and financed emissions

    • ANZ publishes climate related disclosures and scenario analyses for its lending portfolios.
    • Commits to aligning parts of the portfolio with net zero pathways over time, particularly in high emitting sectors.
    • Engages with customers on decarbonisation plans and may adjust financing terms or exposure over time.

    Social factors and financial inclusion

    • Provides basic banking services including low fee accounts and hardship support.
    • Runs programmes aimed at improving financial capability and literacy for customers and communities.
    • Implements frameworks for supporting vulnerable customers and addressing financial abuse risks.

    Governance and transparency

    • Issues annual sustainability or ESG reports alongside financial disclosures.
    • Reports on progress against climate and social commitments and regulatory remediation actions.
    • Faces ongoing investor engagement on topics such as fossil fuel exposure, executive remuneration and board oversight.
