    About the finance and banking sector

    The finance and banking sector includes retail and business banking, insurance, asset management, superannuation, investment banking and payment services. These institutions manage savings, provide credit, allocate capital and underwrite risk across the wider economy.

    Financial institutions play a central role in sustainability because they decide which activities receive funding and on what terms. Their lending, investment and underwriting decisions influence emissions trajectories, natural resource use, social outcomes and the stability of the financial system itself.

    What this section covers

    The finance and banking section of the database focuses on companies that:

    • Provide day to day banking services such as transaction accounts, deposits, mortgages and small business lending.
    • Offer institutional lending and project finance to sectors including energy, infrastructure, property and resources.
    • Manage funds and investment products, including superannuation and wealth management platforms.
    • Underwrite insurance products for households, businesses, property and climate related risks.
    • Operate payment systems, digital wallets and card networks that support the broader economy.

    Key sustainability factors we assess

    When reviewing finance and banking institutions, the database pays particular attention to:

    • Lending and investment policies covering fossil fuels, deforestation, sensitive industries and social safeguards.
    • Climate risk management, including alignment with net zero scenarios, financed emissions targets and exposure to high carbon sectors.
    • Governance and accountability, board oversight of ESG risks, executive incentives and transparency in reporting.
    • Customer treatment and conduct including remediation history, fees, product design and vulnerability safeguards.
    • Financial inclusion and access to basic banking services for underserved communities.
    • Data privacy and cyber security for customers using digital banking and payment services.
