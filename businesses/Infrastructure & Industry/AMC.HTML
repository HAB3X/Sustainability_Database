    Company overview

    Amcor plc is a global packaging company that designs, develops and produces packaging for food, beverages, pharmaceuticals, medical devices, home and personal care and a wide range of other consumer and industrial products. The company specialises in flexible packaging, rigid containers, closures, specialty cartons and high barrier materials for sensitive and sterile products.

    Originating in Australia, Amcor has transformed into a multinational group domiciled in Jersey and based in Zurich, with shares listed in Australia and the United States. Its packaging is used by many of the largest fast moving consumer goods and healthcare companies in the world, which makes Amcor a central player in global supply chains and in debates about plastic, waste and sustainable packaging.

    Business segments

    Flexibles

    • Largest segment by revenue, producing films, pouches, sachets and laminates.
    • Supplies high barrier flexible packaging for food, beverage, pet food, medical and pharmaceutical products.
    • Develops recyclable mono material structures and reduced material formats.

    Rigid packaging

    • Primarily polyethylene terephthalate containers for beverages and other liquids.
    • Focus on lightweight containers that reduce resin usage while maintaining performance.
    • Used for carbonated soft drinks, water, juices and some personal care products.

    Speciality cartons and closures

    • Cartons for sensitive applications and specialist markets.
    • Closures and sealing systems, including tamper evident and child resistant solutions.
    • Medical and healthcare related packs that combine barriers, sterility and usability.

    Global presence

    Amcor operates more than 225 manufacturing and packaging facilities across over 40 countries, spanning Europe, North America, Latin America, Asia Pacific and Africa. This network allows the company to supply major multinational customers in multiple regions and to respond to local regulatory requirements and product expectations.

    Sustainability and key practices

    Packaging design and circularity

    • Public commitment that all Amcor packaging will be recyclable or reusable by 2025.
    • Investment in research and development to create recyclable flexible packaging that can meet food safety and shelf life requirements.
    • Lightweighting programmes for rigid containers to reduce resin usage and transport emissions.
    • Development of products that incorporate recycled content where technically and legally possible.

    Climate and emissions

    • Long term ambition to reach net zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050.
    • Targets to reduce operational emissions intensity and increase procurement of renewable electricity.
    • Energy efficiency projects across plants, including process improvements and equipment upgrades.

    Waste and resource management

    • Internal programmes to reduce production scrap and increase material yield.
    • Participation in industry coalitions and initiatives focused on recycling systems and plastic waste reduction.
    • Support for local recycling and collection schemes in partnership with customers and industry groups.

    Social and governance factors

    People and labour

    • Workforce of about 77 000 employees across production, engineering, design and support functions.
    • Policies and codes that address health and safety, human rights, diversity and inclusion.
    • Publication of modern slavery statements and supplier expectations in relation to labour standards.

    Governance and risk

    • Listed on both the Australian Securities Exchange and the New York Stock Exchange with global investor oversight.
    • Risk management processes cover raw material price volatility, supply chain continuity, regulatory change and reputational risk.
    • Board and executive leadership are responsible for overseeing sustainability strategy and climate related risks.

    Sustainability concerns and gaps

    • Heavy reliance on plastic based packaging, including flexible materials that are difficult to recycle in many markets.
    • Progress toward fully recyclable or reusable packaging commitments varies by region and product line.
    • Global recycling infrastructure is uneven, which limits real world recyclability of some Amcor formats even where designs are technically recyclable.
    • Disclosure on Scope 3 emissions is less detailed than some stakeholders would prefer given the scale of downstream impacts.
    • Healthcare and medical packaging poses particular challenges for recyclability due to sterility and contamination requirements.
