    About the infrastructure and industry sector

    The infrastructure and industry section of this database covers listed companies that provide essential services, heavy industrial capacity and technical capabilities that support the wider economy. These businesses often operate capital intensive assets, complex supply chains and long lived infrastructure with significant environmental and social impacts.

    This sector includes utilities and energy suppliers, fuel refining and distribution, industrial laboratories and testing services, and large scale packaging and materials producers. Their operations are central to transport, energy security, manufacturing, construction and environmental monitoring, which places them at the centre of climate transition planning and regulatory scrutiny.

    What this section covers

    Infrastructure and industry entries in the database focus on companies that:

    • Operate critical energy and utility networks such as electricity generation, gas, and large scale fuel supply.
    • Run refineries, terminals, pipelines and other facilities that store and move resources at national scale.
    • Provide scientific testing, inspection, certification and laboratory services that underpin regulation and safety.
    • Produce industrial packaging and materials that are used across food, beverage, medical and consumer sectors.
    • Have significant climate, pollution, occupational safety and community impact profiles.

    Key sustainability factors we assess

    When reviewing infrastructure and industrial companies, the database considers factors such as:

    • Climate strategy and transition planning for high emissions activities including power generation, refining and heavy industrial production.
    • Operational emissions and energy efficiency across plants, pipelines, refineries, laboratories and manufacturing sites.
    • Resource use and pollution including air emissions, water use, waste, spills and contamination risks.
    • Product and service impacts such as the downstream emissions from sold fuels or the lifecycle of packaging products.
    • Health, safety and labour conditions in high risk environments like refineries, terminals and industrial laboratories.
    • Governance quality, board oversight and disclosure of climate and environmental risks.
    • Community and land impacts related to infrastructure corridors, heavy industry and major energy projects.

    AGL Energy Limited

    AGL Energy Limited is a major Australian electricity and gas supplier with a mix of coal, gas, renewables and storage assets. The company is central to the electricity transition in the National Electricity Market and is assessed on closure plans for coal assets, investment in renewables, customer energy services and governance of climate risk.
