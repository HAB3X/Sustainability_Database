    Company overview

    Ampol Limited is a major Australian based fuel supplier that purchases, refines, imports, distributes and markets transport fuels and related products. The company also operates a large convenience retail network and is expanding into electric vehicle charging and broader energy solutions. Ampol supplies petrol, diesel, jet fuel, marine fuels, lubricants and specialty products to households, businesses and governments.

    The business was previously known as Caltex Australia and changed its name to Ampol in 2020 following the end of its licence agreement with Chevron. Ampol presents itself as both a provider of essential fuel infrastructure and a participant in the energy transition, with operations spanning Australia, New Zealand, Singapore and the United States.

    Business segments

    Fuels and infrastructure

    • Responsible for sourcing, importing, refining and distributing crude oil and refined fuels.
    • Operates Lytton Refinery in Brisbane, one of the last large scale refineries in Australia.
    • Includes trading and shipping activities, terminals, depots, pipelines and other storage infrastructure.
    • Supplies fuels and lubricants to defence, mining, transport, agriculture, aviation, government and industrial customers.

    • Operates Ampol branded service stations and convenience sites across Australia.
    • Combines fuel sales with convenience retail formats such as Ampol Foodary.
    • Acts as a key customer facing channel and brand presence.

    New Zealand (Z Energy)

    • Owns Z Energy, a major fuel retailer and distributor in New Zealand.
    • Operates service stations, truck stops and commercial fuel supply in the New Zealand market.
    • Provides a platform for regional growth in the wider New Zealand fuel sector.

    • Develops and operates AmpCharge branded electric vehicle charging infrastructure.
    • Provides fleet charging, energy management and transition solutions for business customers.
    • Explores opportunities in renewable fuels, hydrogen projects and lower carbon liquid fuels.

    Operations and infrastructure

Lytton Refinery remains a central asset in the portfolio, supported by imported refined product sourced from regional markets. The company also holds strategic stocks under fuel security arrangements with the Australian Government.

    Key risks and concerns

    • Fossil fuel transition risk: core business remains centred on petrol, diesel and aviation fuel at a time of global decarbonisation, which may affect demand over time.
    • Refining margin volatility: profitability of Lytton Refinery is sensitive to global refining margins, crude spreads and policy settings.
    • Regulatory scrutiny: subject to increasing requirements on emissions, fuel quality, safety and reporting standards.
    • Demand shift from electric vehicles: electric vehicle uptake is expected to reduce long term demand for petrol and diesel, requiring a shift in business model towards convenience and new energy services.
    • Scope 3 emissions: most emissions arise from customers burning Ampol‚Äôs fuels, and disclosure and strategy for these downstream emissions are areas of ongoing stakeholder interest.
