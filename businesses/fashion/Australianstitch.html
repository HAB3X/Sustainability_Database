    Company overview

    Australian Stitch is a Melbourne based clothing maker and retailer that focuses on ethical small batch basics. The business operates a retail shopfront in Fitzroy and manufactures locally in Thornbury with a small team. Its range includes classic cotton tees, heavyweight box tees, hoodies, jumpers, and other everyday essentials designed to last.

    The brand was created to support local and ethical manufacturing and to give customers a clear choice away from fast fashion. Australian Stitch invites shoppers to think about the ethical, environmental, social, and economic impacts of the clothes they buy and to choose garments that reflect those values.

    Our story and products

    Australian Stitch describes itself as a home of Australian made basics. Every piece is made in Australia, supporting the makers, craftspeople, and communities involved in production. The stated purpose is to create high quality basics that customers can feel confident wearing and that do not fall apart after one wash.

    The product range centres on blank tees, shorts, jumpers, and tracksuit pieces that are designed to be worn repeatedly. Fabrics include soft breathable materials such as premium cotton and bamboo, chosen for comfort, durability, and lower impact production. Garments are cut and constructed to hold their shape and move comfortably with the body.

    Customers can shop online or visit the Fitzroy store to feel the fabrics and select fit with support from the in store team. Whether online or in person, the aim is to provide the same level of transparency and care around how each garment is made.

    Sustainability and key practices

    • Locally manufactures all apparel in Melbourne using small batch production rather than high volume fast fashion models.
    • Commits to fair wages, safe working conditions, reasonable hours, and equal opportunity employment for staff in line with Australian labour standards.
    • Focuses on durable high quality basics that are intended to be worn for a long time, reducing wardrobe turnover and textile waste.
    • Works with regional suppliers including local dye houses and fabric producers to support the Australian rag trade and shorten supply chains.
    • Encourages customers to consider the ethical, environmental, social, and economic impacts of their clothing choices and to select local options where possible.
