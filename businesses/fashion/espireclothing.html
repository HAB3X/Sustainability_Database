    Company overview

    Espire Clothing is an Australian apparel label that focuses on customisable basics and local manufacturing. The brand positions itself as Australian made, with fabrics sourced through domestic partners and garments stitched in regional Victorian towns such as Bright and Mansfield. The business model centres on small batch production, custom fit, and long lasting everyday pieces rather than high turnover fashion.

    Public material describes a circular design approach framed as seed to store, with emphasis on traceable fibres, regional mills, and on shore production. The brand uses sustainability language prominently, although it currently presents this through narrative case studies at product level rather than through a formal impact report.

    Operations and locations

    • Primary stitching and retail: Bright, Victoria
    • Additional stitching: Mansfield, Victoria
    • Fabric weaving and dyeing: Melbourne, Victoria
    • Supply partners: References to Bendigo wool, Belgian linen, and Good Earth Cotton in product descriptions.

    Our story and product range

    Espire Clothing presents itself as an Australian made label that respects the Traditional Custodians of the land where it operates. The story on its website links each garment to specific mills, farms, and sewing rooms in order to highlight regional skills and local employment. Customers are encouraged to value garments for their provenance and longevity rather than price alone.

    The range includes shirts, linen pieces, customisable hoodies and trackwear, along with a zero waste line known as The Bad Batch that uses off cuts and misprints. Products are described as designed for everyday wear and built to last, with attention to fit and comfort.

    • Shirts including a Belgian linen line.
    • Crew necks and tracksuit pieces using Australian cotton.
    • The Bad Batch zero waste collection that repurposes fabric off cuts.

    Materials and provenance

    • Belgian linen shirts use flax grown in Belgium with rainwater and sun drying, with fabric woven and dyed in Melbourne before stitching in Bright and Mansfield.
    • Crew neck styles use Good Earth Cotton grown in Australia, with knitting and dyeing in Melbourne and final construction in Bright.
    • Product pages frequently reference Melbourne fabric as a marker of local mill involvement.

    Sustainability and key practices

    • Circular seed to store framing that links fibre origin, fabric mills, and sewing rooms and promotes zero waste through The Bad Batch line.
    • On shore manufacturing in Victoria with references to long term supplier relationships and local employment.
    • Explicit acknowledgement of Traditional Custodians of the land and commitment to respectful collaboration with local communities.
    • Use of product level sustainability descriptions to explain fibre choice, water use, and transport distances.
