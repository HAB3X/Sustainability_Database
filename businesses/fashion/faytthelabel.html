    Company overview

    FAYT The Label is an Australian womenswear brand founded by Brittney Saunders in Newcastle. The company focuses on inclusive sizing and community oriented fashion, offering clothing in sizes 6 to 26. It operates both an online store and a growing network of physical locations across Australia.

    The brand positions itself as body positive and accessible, with an emphasis on everyday pieces that can be worn across a range of body types. Marketing material highlights a loyal community, rapid business growth, and a focus on fit, comfort, and confidence for customers who are often underserved by conventional sizing ranges.

    Operations and locations

    • Flagship site: 30 Downie Street, Maryville NSW 2293, within the Newcastle area.
    • Other stores: Westfield Miranda in New South Wales, Westfield Chermside in Queensland, and a store in Hawthorn, Victoria.
    • Online presence: National and international shipping from the main e commerce site.

    Sector and offerings

    • Sector: womens fashion and apparel.
    • Range: tops, dresses, denim, activewear, sets, and basics in sizes 6 to 26.
    • Positioning: inclusive, body positive, and community centred, with an online first model and strong retail expansion.
    • Target market: women seeking fashion forward pieces with extended sizing and consistent fit.

    Sustainability and ethical practices

    • Partners with Thread Together to donate excess stock, samples, and seconds, which supports clothing redistribution and reduces textile waste.
    • Describes use of family owned factories in Bali and China, with stated zero tolerance for child labour, discrimination, or unsafe conditions.
    • Runs a Samples and Seconds collection that sells slightly imperfect garments at a discount, extending the life of products that might otherwise be written off.
    • Publishes general statements on ethical sourcing and factory welfare, including commitments to fair work, safe environments, and responsible manufacturing.
