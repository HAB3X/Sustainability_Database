    Company overview

    AGL Energy Limited is a large integrated energy company supplying electricity, gas and other essential services across Australia. Through its Customer Markets segment it retails electricity and gas, broadband, mobile and voice services, and offers solar and energy efficiency products. It also runs contact centres and customer support operations.

    The Integrated Energy segment manages a substantial generation portfolio that includes coal and gas fired power stations, renewable assets such as wind, hydro and solar, and an emerging fleet of grid scale batteries and gas storage. AGL presents itself as an essential services provider that aims to support customers as they decarbonise and electrify the way they live, work and move.

    Operations and business structure

    • Customer Markets: Retail electricity and gas, broadband, mobile and voice services, solar and energy efficiency products, marketing and customer contact operations.
    • Integrated Energy: Operation of power generation assets across coal, gas, renewables and batteries, along with gas storage, production and energy trading activities.
    • Investments: Holdings and partnerships in energy related ventures and development projects.

    The generation portfolio includes large coal fired power stations at Bayswater in New South Wales and Loy Yang A in Victoria, along with gas peaking plants, hydro schemes, contracted wind and solar farms and grid scale batteries. A high proportion of AGL‚Äôs current operational emissions come from coal combustion at these coal assets.

    Sustainability and key practices

    Decarbonisation and portfolio shift

    • Climate Transition Action Plan linking coal closure dates with emissions reduction targets and new renewable and storage investment.
    • Development of large scale renewable projects and grid scale batteries, supported by long term offtake agreements with wind and solar developers.
    • Reallocation of capital away from some legacy renewable equity holdings while maintaining contracted renewable supply.

    Carbon neutral and customer offerings

    • Climate Active certified carbon neutral gas product with a published disclosure statement covering emissions boundaries and offsets.
    • Customer programmes that promote electrification, efficiency and lower emissions technologies in homes and businesses.

    Customer and social initiatives

    • Support for customers experiencing vulnerability through hardship arrangements and tailored payment support.
    • Focus on keeping essential energy services accessible while the system transitions to lower emissions technologies.

    Risk and involvement assessment

    • Transition and stranded asset risk: financial outcomes are sensitive to coal closure timing, policy settings, technology costs and delivery of new projects.

    • Workforce and community impacts: job reductions linked to the transition programme present social risks, particularly for regional communities that host generation assets.

    Key concerns and improvement opportunities

    • Significant minority opposition to successive climate transition plans suggests investor concern about alignment with one point five degree pathways and the speed of coal exit.
    • Emissions reductions depend heavily on timely closure of coal plants and successful delivery of planned renewable and storage capacity.
    • Use of offsets and framing of carbon neutral products need clear explanation, with stronger emphasis on absolute emissions reduction to maintain trust.
    • Further detail on workforce transition, retraining and regional economic diversification would strengthen the social dimension of the transition plan.
