About the Sustainability Database

    The Sustainability Database is a free, open source project that compiles factual profiles of businesses across Australia and New Zealand. It is designed for people who want to understand how companies operate, rather than relying on marketing language or unverified branding.

    Each entry focuses on location, sector, key practices, risks, and governance, supported where possible by primary sources. The aim is not to promote or condemn individual businesses, but to provide enough structured information for users to make their own judgements.

    The project is maintained using GitHub, with research notes, sources, and changes tracked in a transparent way. You can return to the home page or browse all current listings via the directory.

    How sustainability is defined here

    Sustainability is treated as the intersection of environmental impact, social responsibility, and long term economic resilience. The focus is on what a business actually does, not what it claims in marketing material.

    Environmental sustainability

    • Designing products and services to be durable, repairable, and less resource intensive.
    • Reducing waste through better design, reuse, recycling, and circular models.
    • Managing emissions, water use, and pollution in a measurable way.
    • Using materials and energy sources with lower long term environmental impact.

    Social responsibility

    • Providing safe, fairly paid, and non exploitative work throughout the supply chain.
    • Offering products and services that do not harm health, dignity, or basic rights.
    • Contributing positively to communities rather than extracting value without regard for consequences.
    • Being transparent about ownership, governance, and decision making structures.

    How businesses are grouped

    For clarity, profiles are organised under a small number of umbrella sector pages. These summarise common issues and link through to individual companies.

    Fashion and consumer goods

    Covers clothing, footwear, accessories, and related consumer products where supply chains, labour standards, and material choices are core issues.

    Infrastructure and industry

    Includes large scale utilities, energy providers, and industrial firms where infrastructure, emissions, and long term environmental impacts are central.

    Finance and capital

    Covers banks, asset managers, and financial institutions that allocate capital and shape which activities are funded or starved of resources.

    Gambling and gaming

    Includes companies whose core business model depends on gambling revenue or gambling-like mechanics, where social harm and regulatory risk are key concerns.
